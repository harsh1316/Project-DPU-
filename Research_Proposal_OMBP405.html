1. Title of the Project

"A Study on the Impact of Digital Marketing Strategies on Consumer Buying Behaviour with Special Reference to Flipkart India Pvt. Ltd."

2. Introduction and Background

The rapid digitization of the Indian economy has fundamentally transformed how businesses communicate with consumers. With over 900 million internet users, India represents one of the largest and fastest-growing digital markets globally. Digital marketing – encompassing social media advertising, search engine optimization, email marketing, content marketing, and influencer collaborations – has become the primary channel for e-commerce platforms to reach, engage, and convert consumers.

Flipkart, India's leading e-commerce platform (founded 2007, acquired by Walmart in 2018 for $16 billion), invests significantly in digital marketing to drive customer acquisition and retention. Understanding how these strategies influence consumer buying behaviour is critical for both academic scholarship and practical marketing management.

3. Problem Statement

Despite the massive investment in digital marketing by Indian e-commerce companies, there is limited empirical research examining how specific digital marketing strategies influence the complete consumer buying journey – from awareness to loyalty – across different demographic segments in emerging markets like Pune.

4. Objectives of the Study

  1. To study the various digital marketing strategies adopted by Flipkart India Pvt. Ltd.
  2. To analyze the impact of social media marketing on consumer awareness and purchase intention.
  3. To examine the influence of personalized digital communication on repeat purchase behaviour.
  4. To identify the most effective digital marketing channels across different demographic segments.
  5. To measure the relationship between digital marketing exposure and consumer trust/brand loyalty.
  6. To provide actionable recommendations to enhance digital marketing effectiveness.

5. Scope of the Study

Topic Coverage:Digital Marketing Strategies (SMM, SEO, Email, Content, Influencer) and Consumer Buying Behaviour
Geographical Scope:Pune City, Maharashtra, India
Time Frame:January 2026 – April 2026 (4 months)
Sample Size:150 respondents
Target Population:Online consumers (age 18–55) who have purchased from Flipkart

6. Research Methodology

Research Design:Descriptive Research
Nature of Study:Quantitative (with qualitative elements)
Primary Data:Structured Questionnaire (Google Forms + Printed)
Secondary Data:Journals, books, industry reports, company documents, websites
Sampling Method:Non-Probability – Convenience Sampling
Sample Size:150 respondents
Statistical Tools:Percentage Analysis, Chi-Square Test, Pearson's Correlation, Mean & SD
Software:SPSS v26, MS Excel 2021

7. Hypotheses

  • H1: There is a significant relationship between social media marketing and consumer purchase intention.
  • H2: There is a significant association between digital marketing channels and age group of consumers.
  • H3: Personalized digital communication significantly influences repeat purchase behaviour.

8. Expected Outcomes

  • Identification of the most effective digital marketing channels for Indian consumers.
  • Empirical evidence of the digital marketing–buying behaviour relationship.
  • Demographic-specific insights for targeted marketing strategies.
  • Actionable recommendations for e-commerce marketing practitioners.
